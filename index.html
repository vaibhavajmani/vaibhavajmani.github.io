Influences

Books that shaped how I think

Not a reading list, just six books that improve how I approach the work.

Thinking in Systems Donella Meadows

Reset how I approach any problem: behaviour before structure, feedback loops before fixes. Taught me that leverage points are almost never where they appear to be.

Supercommunicators Charles Duhigg

Made me pay attention to what kind of conversation is actually happening, practical, emotional, or social, before responding. Explained why some meetings resolve and others repeat. Sharpens work with technical and business audiences in the same room.

You're Not Listening Kate Murphy

Reset how I run requirements conversations: listening is the work, not the setup. Argues that most misunderstandings are unheard people, not unclear people. Changed the quality of my calls.

The Signal and the Noise Nate Silver

Distinguishes what a number is telling you from what you want it to tell you. Made me slower with data and quicker to say "I don't know yet." Useful before any dashboard is built.

Unreasonable Hospitality Will Guidara

The idea that service is a design discipline, not a personality trait. Applies as much to internal stakeholders as to guests. Shaped how I frame handoffs, documentation, and responses.

Influence Robert Cialdini

A field guide to why people say yes. Useful to notice when it's being done to you and when you're doing it, the honest version of stakeholder work.
